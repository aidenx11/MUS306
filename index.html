WinterBlue

Aiden Schroeder — MUS 306, 2026

Program Notes

WinterBlue is an ambient piece inspired by the works of Brian Eno and C418.

The melody was generated using the Koenig algorithm to produce a sequence of pitches, which was then manually adjusted. Similarly, the pitches for a custom bell sound heard in the background of the first half of the piece were generated using the same algorithm. The bell sound itself was created using Karplus-Strong synthesis and manipulated in Audacity.

Heard midway through the piece, a texture of clicks and rain-like sounds were created using a Markov chain to determine timing and density, with the resulting material rendered through granular synthesis in a custom script. These sounds were then passed through two different reverbs and waveshapers to vary their timbre.

The pad and bass sounds throughout the piece are based on built-in instrument presets that were processed through waveshapers. The bass sound passes through a low pass filter with automation applied to vary it throughout the piece. Nearly all elements in the piece have some form of reverb applied, which was used to create a sense of shared space.

A wind-like texture runs throughout the piece and grows louder toward the end. This sound was made from a recording of a garbage truck outside, which was then filtered and manipulated in Audacity.

Bio

Aiden Schroeder is a senior studying Computer Science who enjoys listening to ambient music. He enrolled in MUS 306 out of curiosity about how music is made from a technical standpoint.

WinterBlue is his first composed piece (except for one in GarageBand when he was nine), and the process of creating it has sparked a renewed interest in music composition. He plans to continue making music on his own in the future.
